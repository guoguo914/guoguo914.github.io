So far, I've lived in Shanghai, Beijing, Minneapolis and Chicago. Here is a brief summary of my time spent in each city.




Previous Next
Shanghai: I grew up in Shanghai. At the time I was born, it was the largest city in China. It still is. What has changed is its scale and probably its scent. With a population of close to 25 million, the city is enormous. There was once a slogan:"一年一个样，三年大变样", which literarily means, “A new city every year, and a completely different city in three years”. Now as an insider-turned outsider who left the city 11 years ago, I can actually "feel" its change every time I go back to visit family.


Previous Next
Beijing: Capital city of China, wher I went to university. Quite a cultural shock to me the first time I set my feet in. Had too many good memories and totally fell in love with the city. Most of my college friends are still working there.


Previous Next
Minneapolis: My first (and longest) stop in the US. Can not say enough good things about the State. Nice place with super nice people! One of the best places in the US or on the planet for life. The State has 15,000 lakes and many MN family has a boat. I went to graduate school there and got my first Master's degree and subsequently worked there for 4 years.
